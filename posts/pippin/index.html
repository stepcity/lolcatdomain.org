Pippin: the 32-bit microprocessor we all deserved

I started getting into the operating system development hobby when I realized just how hard it is to bring up a complete system on x86 processors — its reliance on the BIOS/UEFI (aside: they both suck!) horribly cripples it. On the other hand, contemporary RISCs — e.g. ARM or RISC-V — either have design flaws (sorry, ARM) or are so underdeveloped that writing assembly causes me physical pain (sorry, RISC-V).

The Motorola 68k series was so incredibly popular for low-cost home computers because it felt powerful and yet didn't bother with anything excessive. At least, through my rose-tinted glasses — in reality, there were a lot of problems on that front, too.

My solution is to make my own. (!!!) I've got a working (although unpublished) spec, and I plan on writing about it here.

Prepare for more posts as I work!
